The Problem

Needs Analysis

Radiation safety training in clinical settings is often passive, forgettable, and ineffective during high-stakes moments. Through firsthand experience and conversations with field professionals, I identified a critical gap: poor decision-making during radioactive material transport, leading to increased regulatory risk.

To address this, I created a game-based learning experience using randomized variables and realistic scenarios. Rather than memorizing guidelines, learners actively apply them—practicing decision-making in simulated conditions that mirror real-world transport challenges. This approach promotes skill transfer and reduces classification errors.

  • Target Audience

    Nuclear Medicine and PET/CT Technologists, and Radioactive Materials Transport Drivers

  • Learning Objective

    Given randomized radiation transport scenarios, learners will analyze Transport Index (TI) and surface radiation levels and apply NRC guidelines to select the correct classification. Mastery is defined as achieving 80% accuracy across multiple trials.

  • ID Skills

    Needs Analysis, SME Consultation, xAPI Data Tracking, Python/Pygame Development, AI Prompt Engineering, PyInstaller Packaging

Design Strategy

Gamification

To reinforce mastery, I designed the game with mechanics that simulate real-world uncertainty and encourage repeated practice. Key features include: randomized scenarios (TI/surface values), scoring and replayability to build fluency, and xAPI tracking to capture learner actions.

I used Adobe Illustrator to create custom game assets, including visuals of a Geiger counter and radioactive package. I also introduced a tiered difficulty system (Easy, Medium, Hard) to promote progressive mastery. The design was inspired by gamification strategist Marie-Jo Leroux, whose framework emphasizes aligning mechanics with instructional goals.

Development

Fully Developed in 2 Weeks With...

To accelerate development, I adapted a pre-built game from Clear Code’s Pygame tutorial and customized it to meet instructional goals. I used prompt engineering techniques to collaborate with AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude, iterating rapidly through coding challenges.

This project significantly deepened my fluency in debugging, logic flow, user input, and Python game deployment. When ChatGPT struggled to resolve a gameplay reset issue, I pivoted to Claude, whose interface proved more efficient for solving that problem. This experience sharpened my technical problem-solving skills and boosted confidence working with AI tools in development workflows.

Evaluation

xAPI Tracking for Learner Data

I integrated full xAPI tracking into the game, connected to Veracity Learning, to capture detailed learner data—name, email, profession, time on task, decisions, and outcomes.

These analytics allow me to evaluate:

  • Player decision accuracy and response patterns
  • Time spent across different game segments
  • Replay behavior and progression over time

I plan to deploy the game to a network of Nuclear Medicine professionals and delivery drivers, then assess engagement and learning performance using these metrics.
